# Project: Incentive Structures Essay

## Goal
Write an essay on optimizing intra-organizational incentive structures, with a specific focus on **incentivizing teams to work together** — particularly how to create joint understanding and shared goals across teams that are working on completely different things.

## Core Thesis (working)
Cross-functional/horizontal alignment is the hardest incentive problem in organizations. Teams with different goals, different outputs, and different metrics will behave like rational game-theory actors and compete for shared resources — even when that's net negative for the organization. The essay should explore how to design incentive structures that remove this dynamic.

## Real-World Anchors
- Northrop Grumman: active example of this problem in a large defense/manufacturing org
- Tesla: stock-based comp as a case study in misaligned long-term incentives
- Patty's manufacturing context: shared equipment/employees across product lines with competing priorities

## Structure (to be developed)
- Start vertically (CEO → hourly employee) by industry type to establish baseline
- Then tackle horizontal/cross-functional alignment as the harder problem
- Compare industry norms: manufacturing vs. consulting vs. big tech
